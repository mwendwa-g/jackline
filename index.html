About Us

Our Mission

Our mission is to raise awareness, provide support, and advocate for individuals and families affected by Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI).We believe that everyone, regardless of their physical challenges, deserves access to the resources, care, and understanding needed to live a fulfilling and empowered life

Our Story

Founded by a passionate group of individuals who have witnessed firsthand the challenges faced by those with brittle bones, the Brittle Bone Agency aims to bridge the gap between awareness and action. Osteogenesis Imperfecta is a rare genetic condition that causes bones to break easily, often with minimal or no impact. Despite affecting thousands of families worldwide, OI remains largely under-recognized in many communities.

What we Do

Raise Awareness: We work to educate the general public, healthcare professionals, and communities about OI, dispelling myths and fostering empathy. We aim to make Osteogenesis Imperfecta a well-known condition, helping reduce stigma and misunderstanding.

Support Families & Individuals: Whether you're newly diagnosed or have been living with OI for years, we offer resources and emotional support to guide you through your journey. From local support groups to expert medical advice, we’re here to help you every step of the way.

Advocate for Better Care: We push for better healthcare policies, improved diagnostic methods, and greater access to treatments for individuals with OI. We are committed to advocating for the needs of the OI community on a local, national, and international level.

Fund Research: We support ongoing research into the causes and treatments of OI. By partnering with scientists, medical professionals, and research organizations, we aim to contribute to groundbreaking discoveries that improve the lives of those with brittle bones.

Our Values

Empathy: understand the challenges of living with a chronic condition, and we approach every interaction with compassion and respect.

Education: We believe in the power of knowledge and work tirelessly to educate others about OI, empowering families and healthcare providers to make informed decisions.

Collaboration: We are stronger together. Our partnerships with medical professionals, advocacy groups, and individuals with OI ensure that we can create a broader impact.

Hope: Despite the challenges of brittle bones, we believe in the power of hope. Every day, we see individuals with OI achieving extraordinary things, and we’re committed to helping make those achievements more possible for everyone in the community

FAQs

What is brittle bone disease (Osteogenesis Imperfecta)

Brittle bone disease, also known as Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI), is a genetic condition that results in fragile bones that break easily, often with little or no apparent cause. People with OI may have bone fractures, growth issues, and sometimes hearing loss, brittle teeth, and joint problems. The severity of OI can vary greatly from person to person.

Is there a cure for brittle bone disease

Currently, there is no cure for brittle bone disease, but treatment can help manage the symptoms. This often includes physical therapy, medications to strengthen bones, and surgeries to treat fractures or bone deformities. Researchers are actively studying new treatments, and early diagnosis can help manage the condition more effectively

Do you offer resources for individuals and families affected by brittle bones

Yes, we provide resources such as educational materials, support groups, and a network of healthcare professionals familiar with brittle bone disease. Our goal is to empower those affected by OI with the tools they need to manage their condition and connect with others in the community.

What are the different types of brittle bone disease (OI)

Type I: Mildest form, with frequent fractures and some issues with bones and joints.
Type II: The most severe form, often resulting in death shortly after birth.
Type III: Severe, but individuals can survive into adulthood with appropriate care.
Type IV: Moderate severity with frequent fractures but typically normal life expectancy.
